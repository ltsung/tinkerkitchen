Allergens

At Tinker Kitchen we understand the importance of allergens, and take steps to control them and avoid cross-contamination. Here’s what you need to know.

We do not operate in allergen-free environments, but if you contact us ahead of your workshop we are able to accommodate most situations (or offer you a full refund). Please write to us at info@tinkerkitchen.org and we will be happy to discuss your requirements.

Pop-up Environment

Our workshops take place in “pop-up” environments. We clean all surfaces and equipment, but other allergens may be present.

For example: pets may be present in homes (even if not in the kitchen – you may feel it).

Note that there is some equipment we do not bring to a pop-up kitchen, such as the fridge or sink. Generally we are able to avoid cross-contamination, but it's important that you let us know so we can plan accordingly.

Equipment and Utensils

Tinker Kitchen takes care to thoroughly clean the specialized equipment and utensils we bring to use during workshops. However, even careful cleaning is not enough to guarantee absence of allergens.

For example: our pasta extrusion machine is regularly used with semolina flour containing gluten, and nuts are sometimes used when making chocolate or ice cream.

Ingredients

If you have a food allergy, chances are you will know what recipes you cannot eat. For example, traditional pasta has gluten, and ice cream has dairy. If you are unsure, however, we can discuss the ingredients in our recipes with you.

In some cases, we can tweak our menus or swap ingredients to suit your requirements. We also take care to avoid cross-contamination of our ingredient sources as much as possible (e.g. we do not allow nuts to touch our stored chocolate), but be aware that we do use the same equipment for all our workshops–so if you have a severe allergy let us know.
